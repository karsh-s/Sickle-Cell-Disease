How is Sickle Cell Disease Inherited? 
    What can cause someone to have Sickle Cell Disease?

The only possible way for a person to get Sickle Cell Disease is if their parents are carriers of the disorders. Both the mother and father may only be carriers and not actually get affected by the disorder. They would have one Hemoglobin A allele (Dominant) and One Hemoglobin S allele (Recessive). From these alleles, only 3 genotypes can be formed: 2 Hemoglobin S alleles, 2 Hemoglobin A alleles or 1 Hemoglobin S allele and 1 Hemoglobin A allele. There is a 50% that the child has only 1 Hemoglobin S allele and 1 Hemoglobin A allele. This means they are only carriers of the genetic mutation. There is a 25% chance that the child has 2 Hemoglobin A alleles. This would mean they don't carry the genetic mutation at all. There is also 25% that the child has 2 Hemoglobin S alleles, which indicates that they have Sickle Cell Disease. What is the Genetic Test for Sickle Cell Disease? 
      How can someone check if they have it?

When a baby is born, they go through a screening process where they have to give a blood test. A small sample of blood is given to the lab to analyse. The blood would go through a High Performance Liquid Chromotography (image on the right). The type of hemoglobin one has can be evaluated and from this genetic test, your result on whether you have Sickle Cell Disease or not can be discovered. Just like babies, adults can take this test too.
